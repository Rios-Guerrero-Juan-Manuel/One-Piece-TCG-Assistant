# One Piece TCG - Strategy Guide

## Deck Building Fundamentals

### Curve Management
A well-balanced deck has a smooth cost curve. Aim for:
- **Cost 0-2**: 15-20 cards (early game presence, blockers, counter)
- **Cost 3-4**: 12-15 cards (mid-game pressure, searchers)
- **Cost 5+**: 8-12 cards (late-game finishers, boss characters)

A deck with too many high-cost cards will struggle in the early game. A deck with too many low-cost cards will run out of steam.

### Role Distribution
Every deck needs a mix of functional roles:
- **Engine/Searchers** (4-8 cards): Cards that draw or search your deck for consistency
- **Blockers** (4-8 cards): Defense against opponent attacks
- **Counter cards** (6-10 cards): +1000 or +2000 counter for combat tricks
- **Removal** (2-4 cards): Cards that can KO or banish opponent's characters
- **Finishers** (2-4 cards): High-power characters or game-ending effects

### Color Synergy
Stick to your Leader's colors. Dual-color Leaders give more flexibility but may dilute your strategy. Mono-color decks are more consistent but have fewer options.

## Early Game (Turns 1-3)

### Priorities
1. **Establish board presence**: Play cost 1-2 Characters immediately
2. **Set up DON ramp**: Ensure you have DON in your Area each turn
3. **Search for key cards**: Use searcher effects to find your engine pieces
4. **Hold counters**: Keep counter cards in hand for defense

### Common Mistakes
- Playing all your DON on one Character instead of spreading across multiple
- Not playing blockers early, leaving your Leader exposed
- Using counter cards when not necessary (wasting resources)
- Not attacking with available Characters (missing pressure opportunities)

## Mid Game (Turns 4-7)

### Priorities
1. **Build board advantage**: Play your 3-4 cost Characters with strong effects
2. **Control the board**: Use removal effects on key opponent Characters
3. **Maintain card advantage**: Keep drawing cards through engine effects
4. **Plan your finish**: Identify what will close out the game

### Key Decisions
- When to attack Leader vs Characters (pressure vs board control)
- Whether to hold DON for a big play next turn or use it now
- Which opponent Character to remove first (prioritize engines and blockers)

## Late Game (Turns 8+)

### Priorities
1. **Close out the game**: Attack with everything available
2. **Protect your board**: Keep counters for crucial attacks
3. **Manage resources**: Don't overcommit if you can win next turn
4. **Watch for topdeck**: If both players are low on cards, every draw matters

## Matchup Tips

### vs Aggro Decks
- Prioritize blockers early
- Don't overextend your board (they want to clear it)
- Save removal for their biggest threat
- Counter efficiently - only counter attacks that would hit your Leader

### vs Control Decks
- Apply early pressure before they set up
- Don't play all your threats at once (bait removal)
- Keep your hand full for the late game
- Their deck gets stronger over time - race them

### vs Midrange Decks
- Mirror match: whoever establishes board first usually wins
- Focus on trade efficiency (don't lose 2-for-1 exchanges)
- Your engine cards matter more than in other matchups
- The player with better card draw usually wins

## DON Management

### Efficient DON Usage
- Turn 1: 0 DON available, play cost 0-1 cards
- Turn 2: 1 DON available, play cost 1-2 cards
- Turn 3: 2 DON available, play cost 2-3 cards
- Turn 4: 3 DON available, play cost 3-4 cards
- Turn 5+: 4+ DON available, play your full curve

### DON Mistakes to Avoid
- Leaving DON unused at end of turn (wasted resources)
- Attaching all DON to one Character (vulnerable to removal)
- Not attaching DON to attackers (missing lethal damage)
- Forgetting to add DON to your Area each turn

## Counter Play

### When to Counter
- Counter when the attack would K.O. a key Character
- Counter when the attack would hit your Leader and you have no Blocker
- Counter to win a crucial combat that swings the board

### When NOT to Counter
- The attack would hit your Leader but you have 4+ Life
- The attack targets a Character you were planning to sacrifice
- You need the counter card for a more important play next turn
- The counter wouldn't change the outcome of the combat

## Trigger Management

### Life Card Strategy
- Your Life cards are your hand-in-waiting. Consider what's in your Life when making decisions.
- If you have strong Triggers in Life, you may play more aggressively.
- If your Life has no Triggers, be more defensive - each Life hit is a card lost without benefit.

### Trigger Priority
When revealing Triggers from Life:
1. **Draw 2**: Best trigger - card advantage
2. **Draw**: Good trigger - replaces itself
3. **Counter**: Situational - good if you need counter
4. **Rest**: Good for disrupting opponent's attack
5. **Fatal**: Best for aggressive decks - extra damage
