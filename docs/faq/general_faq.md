# One Piece TCG - Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### Q: What is the optimal deck size?
A: Exactly 50 cards. You cannot have fewer or more than 50 cards in your main deck (excluding the Leader).

### Q: Can I have more than 4 copies of a card?
A: Only if the card text explicitly states "you may have any number of this card in your deck." Otherwise, the maximum is 4 copies per card number.

### Q: What happens if my deck runs out of cards?
A: If you cannot draw a card when required (start phase draw or card effect), you lose the game immediately.

### Q: Can I look at my trash during the game?
A: Yes, both players can look at either trash at any time. The trash is public information.

## Combat Questions

### Q: Can I attack with multiple characters in one turn?
A: Yes, you can attack with any number of your Characters during your Attack Phase, as long as they are in active (standing) position.

### Q: What happens when two Characters have equal power in combat?
A: Both Characters are trashed (K.O.'d) simultaneously. This is called a "double KO."

### Q: Can I use Blocker on an attack targeting my Character?
A: No. Blocker only works when your Leader is being attacked. It cannot intercept attacks targeting other Characters.

### Q: Does Rush work if the Character is played from trash?
A: Yes. Rush allows the Character to attack the turn it enters play, regardless of where it was played from.

## Don Questions

### Q: When can I add Don to my Don Area?
A: Once per turn during your Main Phase, you may place 1 Don from the top of your deck into your Don Area in rested position.

### Q: Can I move Don between Characters?
A: No. Once Don is attached to a Character or Leader, it stays there until that card leaves play. You cannot reassign Don.

### Q: What happens to Don when a Character is trashed?
A: All Don attached to that Character returns to your Don Area. The Don cards are not trashed.

### Q: Does Player 2 really start with 1 Don?
A: Yes. Player 2 (the player who goes second) starts the game with 1 Don card already in their Don Area. This is the compensation for going second.

## Trigger Questions

### Q: What happens when I reveal a Trigger from Life?
A: Activate the Trigger effect immediately, then place the card in your hand (unless the effect says otherwise).

### Q: Can I choose not to activate a Trigger?
A: No. Trigger effects are mandatory. You must resolve them when revealed from Life.

### Q: What if I reveal multiple Triggers from one attack?
A: Some effects (like Double Attack/Fatal) can cause multiple Life cards to be revealed. Resolve each Trigger in order, from top of deck first.

## Card Effect Questions

### Q: What does "look at top X cards of your deck" mean?
A: You look at the specified number of cards from the top of your deck, choose some to keep (if the effect allows), and return the rest to the top or bottom of your deck as specified.

### Q: Can I activate a Leader ability multiple times per turn?
A: Only if the ability does not specify a limit. Many Leader abilities say "[Once Per Turn]" which means you can only activate it once per turn.

### Q: What does "rest" mean?
A: Rotating a card 90 degrees to indicate it has been used. Rested Characters cannot attack or use Blocker. Cards refresh (become active/standing) during your start phase.

### Q: What is the difference between "trash" and "banish"?
A: Trashed cards go to your discard pile and can be retrieved by effects. Banished cards are removed from the game entirely and cannot be retrieved.

## Format Questions

### Q: What is the Western format?
A: The official format used in international tournaments. Uses the current ban list published by Bandai.

### Q: Can I use cards from different sets in the same deck?
A: Yes, as long as the cards are not banned in your chosen format and match your Leader's colors.

### Q: What are banned pairs?
A: Certain combinations of cards that cannot be in the same deck together. For example, if Card A and Card B are a banned pair, you cannot include both in your deck.
